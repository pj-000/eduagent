# 📄 Lesson Plan: Simplifying Algebraic Expressions  
*Grade 9 • Mathematics • Duration: 45 min*

## 🎯 Learning Objective  
> Students will simplify algebraic expressions by applying the distributive property and combining like terms.

## 📚 Prerequisite Knowledge (per EduOnto-DM v2.1)  
- Variables as placeholders (`v2.1#L7#variables_and_terms`)  
- Integer addition/subtraction rules (`v2.1#L8#basic_operations_with_integers`)  

## 🧩 Lesson Flow  

### ⏱ Warm-up (5 min)  
> *Diagnostic prompt:*  
> “Which of these are like terms? Why or why not?  
> a) $3x$ and $5x^2$  
> b) $-2ab$ and $7ba$  
> c) $4$ and $-9y$”  

### 😊 Direct Instruction (15 min)  
- Visual animation: *Distributive Property as Area Model*  
- Step-by-step simplification of $-3(2x - 4) + 5x$  
- Common error spotlight: coefficient vs. exponent confusion  

### 🤝 Guided Practice (12 min)  
- Collaborative whiteboard: simplify $4(y - 2) - 2(3y + 1) + y$  
- Peer feedback rubric: “Did you distribute to *all* terms inside parentheses?”  

### 🚪 Exit Ticket (8 min)  
> *Single-question diagnostic:*  
> Simplify: $2a(b - 3) - a(4 - b)$  
> Show all steps. Circle your final simplified expression.

## 📎 Resources  
- Video: [Distributive Property Explained — 3 min animated](https://cdn.edu.gov.cn/videos/v2.1/L9/distributive-area-model.mp4)  
- Interactive Desmos activity: [Like Terms Sorter](https://teacher.desmos.com/activitybuilder/custom/abc123def456)  
- Printable worksheet (PDF): `MATH-L9-AE-WKST-1.2.pdf`
